A Little About Us

Kenya, often referred to as the jewel of East Africa, boasts a rich agricultural heritage, with tea being one of its most valuable exports. Covering vast highland areas with fertile volcanic soil, the country enjoys a favorable climate characterized by moderate temperatures, reliable rainfall, and well-distributed rivers—ideal conditions for tea cultivation.

With these natural advantages, the tea sector has remained a cornerstone of Kenya’s economy, significantly contributing to foreign exchange earnings, employment, and rural development. Small-scale tea farmers, supported by the Kenya Tea Development Agency (KTDA), form the backbone of the industry, producing high-quality tea that is renowned worldwide.

KTDA plays a crucial role in overseeing the processing, marketing, and distribution of tea while ensuring that farmers receive technical support, training, and fair returns for their produce. The agency's commitment to sustainable agricultural practices and innovation continues to enhance the livelihoods of thousands of farmers and drive the growth of the tea industry in Kenya.
